  Creating Digital Video
  
 

Digital Video involved choosing two different kinds of videos and then examining them to see how they were fit for purpose and the effect that they had on the audience. It was really about learning about other peoples videos so that we could then use what we had learnt in the design of our own creation.

We designed the video using a storyboard technique that had images of the intended scenes in the game and a description of what we intended to put at that point of the video. So in the end we were left with basically a comic strip of the video you intend to create.

I had intended to do a walkthrough of a game called SpongeBob but the switch computer I was using caused me problems so I had to abandon the idea. But as a result I produced two images of the main characters in the game SpongeBob and Patrick Sponge Bob's friend. I drew the image outlines on paper then I scanned them into the computer and coloured them in photoshop.

I chose to do a video that would be an introduction to Crown Green Bowls. The intention was to provide people with a taster about playing Crown Green Bowls.

I did find that the vision I had about what I would be able to put in the video was different from the content that went into the video. While I did plan quite well using the storyboard technique I did realise that in future I would plan it out more thoroughly and I would use a script and record all the audio first then I would know how much video was needed for each section of the video. Because one part of the video left out was about how to hold a bowl would have taken me 10 to 15 minutes to describe and the video was to be 10 to 15 minutes long in its entirety.

I used Adobe Premiere to compile the video that included the video clips, a sound track, on screen text titles, images and the spoken audio that was recorded separately.

The audio that was recorded separately was recorded using a program called Audacity that is free to use.

The audio that was recorded with the video clips was not usable because of the background noise that included wind, birds, cars and building work. The only audio I kept in was the introduction that was me introducing the video and myself. This is because I didn't want to have to sync the audio to the video clips.


The finished video can be viewed at: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gcDYstbYh38
