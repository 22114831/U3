  Computer Networking  
     
 

On this course we were taught basic networking that allowed us to identify components used in networking and their uses like switches that are usually the backbone of the network. It also allowed us to identify different types of networks like LAN, WAN and PeertoPeer networks. We looked at home networks and office networks and the differences between them. We also looked at types of connections like wired and wireless. We  learn about internet protocols and how data is transferred over networks including the internet, also about how data is manipulated over networks. We learnt about the structure of the client server network. We learnt about creating user accounts and setting user restrictions and management. We learnt about security issues on the network like viruses and hacking and security measures to protect the network. We learnt about Topologies and Physical connections, and different types of cabling available. We looked at productivity and user experience. 

We had to review how the uses and features of two different computer networks affect the user  Experience. We looked at different computer networks and the first was our own home network and we described how everything was connected and its purpose on the network. We then looked at a typical office network and then compared the two networks identifying and differences between them. We looked at advantages and disadvantages of different types of networks including WANS, LANS, Peer-To-Peer. We also looked at advantages and disadvantages of network components. We used  Packet Tracer to create two networks. One network was our home network with our devices connected to it and the other was a Peer-To-Peer network.
